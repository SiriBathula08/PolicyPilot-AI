PM Mudra Yojana
The Pradhan Mantri Mudra Yojana (PMMY) is an Indian government scheme launched in 2015 to provide financial support to micro and small enterprises. It aims to promote entrepreneurship and self-employment by offering collateral-free loans to non-corporate, non-farm small businesses.

Key facts
Launch year: 2015

Implementing agency: Micro Units Development and Refinance Agency (MUDRA) Ltd.

Loan types: Shishu, Kishor, and Tarun

Maximum loan amount: ₹10 lakh

Collateral requirement: None

Objectives and scope
PM Mudra Yojana was designed to empower micro-entrepreneurs and bring informal businesses into the formal financial system. It supports individuals engaged in manufacturing, trading, and service activities, including small retailers, artisans, and vendors. The scheme’s goal is to strengthen the foundation of the “non-corporate small business sector,” which plays a major role in job creation in India.

Loan categories
The scheme offers three loan categories based on the stage and funding needs of the enterprise:

Shishu: Up to ₹50,000 for startups and early-stage businesses.

Kishor: Between ₹50,001 and ₹5 lakh for growing enterprises.

Tarun: Between ₹5 lakh and ₹10 lakh for expansion-ready businesses.
These loans are disbursed through banks, microfinance institutions, and non-banking financial companies (NBFCs).

Implementation and impact
MUDRA Ltd., a subsidiary of the Small Industries Development Bank of India, manages refinancing and monitors lending institutions. The scheme has disbursed billions of rupees to millions of borrowers, particularly benefiting women and first-time entrepreneurs. It contributes to the government’s broader initiative of inclusive economic growth and financial inclusion.

Eligibility and process
Eligible borrowers include small businesses, shopkeepers, vendors, and other micro-entrepreneurs. Applicants can approach participating financial institutions with a business plan; upon approval, the loan is credited directly to their account. Interest rates and repayment terms vary by lender but remain competitive to encourage uptake.
