Skill India Mission
The Skill India Mission is a national initiative launched by the Government of India in 2015 to equip youth with employable skills across diverse sectors. It seeks to create a skilled workforce aligned with industry needs and boost economic productivity, self-employment, and entrepreneurship.

Key facts
Launch year: 2015

Implementing body: Ministry of Skill Development and Entrepreneurship (MSDE)

Flagship program: Pradhan Mantri Kaushal Vikas Yojana (PMKVY)

Target audience: Indian youth aged 15–45

Objective: Train over 400 million people by 2022

Background and objectives
Skill India Mission was introduced under the leadership of Narendra Modi to address India’s skill gap and high youth unemployment. It consolidates multiple skill development initiatives into one coordinated framework, emphasizing demand-driven training, certification, and industry linkage.

Core components
The mission integrates several schemes and agencies, including the National Skill Development Corporation (NSDC) and the Directorate General of Training (DGT). Its main components are short-term training under PMKVY, apprenticeship promotion, recognition of prior learning, and the establishment of model training centers known as “Pradhan Mantri Kaushal Kendras.”

Implementation and impact
Through sector skill councils and training partners, Skill India has trained millions of individuals in trades such as construction, healthcare, information technology, and manufacturing. Emphasis on quality assurance, digital learning, and rural outreach supports both formal employment and entrepreneurship. The mission complements programs like Make in India and Digital India to strengthen human capital and economic growth.

Current status
Ongoing reforms aim to improve outcome-based training and international mobility of Indian workers. Recent phases of PMKVY focus on industry-aligned curricula, district-level skill hubs, and partnerships with states to enhance accessibility and job linkages.
