## Pradhan Mantri Awas Yojana

Pradhan Mantri Awas Yojana (PMAY) is a flagship housing scheme of the Government of India launched in 2015 to provide affordable housing to all urban and rural citizens. It aims to ensure “Housing for All” by facilitating financial assistance and subsidies for constructing, purchasing, or renovating homes.

### Key facts

* **Launch year:** 2015
* **Implemented by:** Ministry of Housing and Urban Affairs / Ministry of Rural Development
* **Sub-schemes:** PMAY-Urban (PMAY-U) and PMAY-Gramin (PMAY-G)
* **Target completion:** Housing for all by 2024
* **Primary benefit:** Interest subsidy up to 6.5% under the Credit Linked Subsidy Scheme (CLSS)

### Objectives and Scope

The scheme seeks to promote affordable housing through direct support to beneficiaries and partnerships with public and private developers. It prioritizes economically weaker sections (EWS), low-income groups (LIG), and certain middle-income categories. PMAY covers both urban and rural regions, addressing issues of housing shortages and substandard living conditions.

### Implementation and Mechanism

PMAY operates through two components: **PMAY-Urban**, focusing on slum redevelopment and credit-linked subsidies, and **PMAY-Gramin**, designed to replace kutcha houses in rural areas with pucca houses. Beneficiaries are identified via the Socio-Economic Caste Census (SECC) data and receive funds directly into their bank accounts in phased installments tied to construction progress.

### Funding and Partnerships

The program is financed jointly by central and state governments, with additional involvement from banks and housing finance companies under the CLSS. Urban local bodies, state housing boards, and private developers also play roles in project execution and land provision.

### Impact and Progress

PMAY has become one of India’s largest social infrastructure programs, with millions of homes sanctioned nationwide. It has stimulated construction employment, improved access to formal housing finance, and contributed to broader goals of urban renewal and rural development.
